    Paper 6.1 → Paper 7 Integration Notes¶

    Extending the Operational Substrate into Cross‑Domain Structural Cognition¶

    Paper 6.1 establishes the Minimal Reference System (MRS) and the Convergence Test Suite (CTS) as the operational substrate of the IBCS architecture.
    Paper 7 builds directly on this substrate to introduce Cross‑Domain Structural Cognition (CDSC) — the ability to project, align, and reason across heterogeneous domains using invariant‑preserving mappings.

    This document explains how the components of Paper 6.1 become the foundation for Paper 7.

    1. From Surfaces to Domains¶

    Paper 6.1 defines surfaces as the observable interfaces of cognition (M0).
    Paper 7 generalizes this concept:

    • A domain is a structured surface.
    • A domain projection is a mapping between surfaces.
    • A cross‑domain alignment is a continuity‑preserving transformation.

    Thus:

    Surface (M0) → Domain (D) Surface mapping → Domain projection Surface coherence → Cross‑domain coherence

    Code

    The MRS surface generator becomes the domain generator in Paper 7.

    2. Reflexive Loop → Structural Update Loop¶

    The reflexive loop in Paper 6.1:

    T' = T - L(T - f(S_i))

    Code

    becomes the structural update loop in Paper 7:

    D' = D - L(D - g(P_j))

    Code

    Where:

    • D is a domain structure
    • Pⱼ is a projection from another domain
    • g extracts structural meaning

    This generalizes reflexive updates into cross‑domain structural updates.

    3. Continuity (M3) → Cross‑Domain Continuity¶

    Paper 6.1 continuity ensures:

    • coherence across surfaces
    • coherence across time
    • coherence across reflexive cycles

    Paper 7 extends this to:

    • coherence across domains
    • coherence across projections
    • coherence across structural transformations

    Continuity becomes the governing invariant of cross‑domain reasoning.

    4. Governance (M4) → Structural Governance¶

    Paper 6.1 governance enforces:

    • safety
    • coherence
    • invariant alignment

    Paper 7 governance enforces:

    • structural validity
    • projection safety
    • cross‑domain consistency
    • prevention of structural drift

    Governance becomes the constraint layer for domain projection systems.

    5. MRS → Structural Cognition Engine (SCE)¶

    The MRS runtime becomes the foundation for the Structural Cognition Engine in Paper 7.

    Mapping:

    MRS Component Paper 7 Equivalent Purpose
    Surfaces Domains Represent structured contexts
    Reflexive Engine Structural Update Engine Update domain structures
    Continuity Engine Cross‑Domain Continuity Engine Maintain coherence across domains
    Governance Layer Structural Governance Layer Enforce constraints
    CTS Structural Validation Suite Validate cross‑domain mappings

    Paper 7 does not replace MRS — it extends it.

    6. CTS → Structural Validation Suite (SVS)¶

    CTS validates:

    • contraction
    • convergence
    • bounded drift
    • invariant alignment

    SVS validates:

    • projection correctness
    • structural alignment
    • cross‑domain coherence
    • invariant preservation across domains

    CTS is the precursor to SVS.

    7. Paper 6.1 → Paper 7 Transition Summary¶

    Paper 6.1 provides:

    • the operational substrate
    • the reflexive engine
    • the continuity engine
    • the governance layer
    • the validation suite

    Paper 7 builds on this to provide:

    • domain structures
    • projection systems
    • structural mappings
    • cross‑domain reasoning
    • structural governance
    • multi‑domain continuity

    This is the transition from operational cognition to structural cognition.

    8. Position in the Canon¶

    Paper 6.1 → Paper 7 is the most important transition in the IBCS stack:

    • Paper 6.1: “How cognition operates.”
    • Paper 7: “How cognition generalizes across domains.”

    This prepares the foundation for:

    • Paper 8 — Domain Projection Systems
    • Paper 9.x — Monasterial Scale, M‑levels, 4RGE fabric
    • Paper 10.x — Applied Systems

    Paper 7 is the bridge from substrate to structure.
